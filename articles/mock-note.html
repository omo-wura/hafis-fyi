The Tragedy of the Source

Published: June 29, 2026 Category: Concepts

This is a placeholder note to showcase the new Note style. By leaving out the "Series" tag, the site builder automatically renders this as a standalone note.

When voltage is viewed as spatial pressure, a resistor is simply a bottleneck. But what happens when that bottleneck isn't an external component, but a physical limitation of the voltage source itself? This is the core tragedy of the source: it can never deliver its full, ideal pressure to the outside world because it must first push charge through its own internal resistance, losing energy to heat before the current even leaves the battery terminals.
