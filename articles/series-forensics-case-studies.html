Forensics Case Studies

Engineering is not just about building things; it is often about tearing them apart when they fail in unexpected ways. In my experience across high-voltage power electronics and fast-charging infrastructure, failures are rarely simple. They are usually the result of cascading edge cases, unpredictable environmental factors, and unforeseen interactions between analog hardware and digital firmware.

This series acts as a collection of forensic case studies. In each entry, I dissect a specific, complex failure mode that I encountered in the field or in the lab. Rather than just presenting the final solution, I walk through the debugging journey: the initial symptoms, the false assumptions, the diagnostic testing, and the first-principles analysis required to finally isolate the root cause.

Whether it's a sporadic desaturation fault in an IGBT module or an unpredictable thermal runaway condition, these stories highlight the critical importance of looking beyond the datasheet and questioning every assumption.
