# Rebuilding the Foundations

* **Date:** June 22, 2026
* **Series:** Electronics from the Bottom Up: A First-Principles Guide
* **Series Part:** 1
* **Category:** Concepts
* **Tags:** Electrical Engineering, Physics, Education
* **Excerpt:** Reconstructing our understanding of electrical engineering by discarding mathematical abstractions and viewing circuits through a charge-centric, physics-first lens.

---

For years, I navigated the complex world of electrical engineering, accumulating educational accomplishments and mastering the intricate mathematics required to design advanced systems. Yet, despite this rigorous training, I hit a wall. Electronics felt mechanical to me but I yearned for a more physical understanding. Traditional electronics education relies too heavily on mathematical abstraction, teaching us to view components as black boxes. Resistors are dividers, capacitors are mathematical integrators, and inductors are differentiators. This served me well for passing exams, but as a consequence, I had memorized the effects before truly understanding their underlying causes. In my career experience as engineer, this abstraction ultimately became a conceptual cage. I realized I was missing a fundamental connection to the very forces I was manipulating.

That realization spurred the creation of this series, *Electronics from the Bottom Up: A First-Principles Guide*.

At school, we are often handed formulas as if they were scripture. We are taught to solve matrices and balance equations, but we lose sight of the physical reality happening within the copper and silicon. I embarked on a journey to reconstruct my understanding of electronics from the ground up, moving away from treating components as abstract mathematical operators and instead viewing them through a charge-centric, physics-first lens.

In this series, we will discard the abstraction and view the circuit as a tangible, physical territory. We will re-examine voltage not merely as a variable in an equation, but as a spatial pressure—a mandate from a power source attempting to exert its will upon a vast crowd of charge carriers. We will explore the "Tragedy of the Source," where the orderly intent of this pressure is squandered as an entropic toll by the resistor, a component that acts as a chaotic, vibrating obstacle course that steals energy as heat.

From there, we will rebuild the passive components into mechanical realities. You will no longer see the capacitor merely as a mathematical derivative of voltage; instead, you will understand it as a "Stretchy Room," a space where energy is hoarded in the physical tension of dielectric atoms leaning into an electric field. We will demystify the inductor by revealing the "Vortex of Lag," showing how a coil of wire acts as a "Heavy Flywheel" that stores energy in the kinetic momentum of a magnetic wake.

Finally, we will unite these concepts into a "Mechanical Symphony," bridging the gap between fundamental solid-state physics and practical AC circuit design. By replacing abstract symbols with a mechanical intuition of tension and momentum, the confusing formulas fade away, leaving a clear, intuitive mastery of electromagnetic fields and particle dynamics.

Whether you are a student struggling to visualize field theory, or a seasoned professional frustrated by the limitations of traditional circuit models, I invite you to join me. Let us rebuild our understanding of electronics from the bottom up.
