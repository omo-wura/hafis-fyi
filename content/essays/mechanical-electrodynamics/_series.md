# Mechanical Electrodynamics: Rebuilding My Electronics Intuition From First Principles

* **Excerpt:** Follow along as I reconstruct my understanding of electronics by putting charge-centric physics before mathematical abstractions

---

Like most engineers, my foundation in electronics was built on mathematically efficient abstractions: Ohm’s Law, Kirchhoff’s Laws, and lumped-element models. Throughout my career, I've recognized that these abstractions are vital for speed, but come with a hidden cost. We begin to view components as black boxes, divorcing electronics from the physical reality of electromagnetic fields, charge distributions, and particle dynamics.

On stepping into high-frequency design, power electronics, and semiconductor physics, I started out viewing wires transforming into waveguides and parasitics emerging from geometry as annoyances to correct in the math. Until I realized I had it backwards. Wires acting as ideal lines should be the exception, not the norm. To sharpen my intuition and ability to reduce complex problems down to what truly matters, I set out to re-learn electronics through the lens of mechanical electrodynamics.

This series of articles represents my journey of rebuilding that intuition from the ground up. The goal is simple: to witness the emergence of unfeeling equations from tangible, physical reality—and to know exactly when to leverage them.
