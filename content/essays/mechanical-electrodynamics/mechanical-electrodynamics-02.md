In our first-principles journey, we re-examine voltage not merely as a variable in an equation, but as a spatial pressure—a mandate from a power source attempting to exert its will upon a vast crowd of charge carriers. 

In this article, we will explore the "Tragedy of the Source," where the orderly intent of this pressure is squandered as an entropic toll by the resistor. A resistor acts as a chaotic, vibrating obstacle course that steals energy as heat. We will look at how charge carriers collide with the atomic lattice, converting electrical potential energy into thermal vibrations, and derive Ohm's law from particle drift velocities.
