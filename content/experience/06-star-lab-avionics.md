# Lead Avionics Developer

* **Organization:** STAR Laboratory, University of Manitoba (CSA CubeSat Project)
* **Title:** Lead Avionics Developer (IRIS 3U CubeSat Team)
* **Period:** September 2019 - December 2020

## Role Overview
Led the hardware architecture, component selection, schematic capture, multi-layer PCB layout, and physical bring-up of the avionics stack for the IRIS 3U research nanosatellite under the Canadian Space Agency's Canadian CubeSat Project.

## Core Accomplishments
* **Multi-MCU Architecture Design:** Selected and integrated a heterogeneous controller architecture to partition payload, power, and flight logic: a Microsemi SmartFusion2 SoC (OBC), an STM32F4 (Imaging Payload), and a TI MSP430 (EPS). Designed the system-level CAN bus communication network linking the boards.
* **Direct Energy Transfer (DET) Power Bus:** Engineered the satellite's EPS power distribution network using a DET architecture and Sequential Switch Regulation (SSR) to manage LiFePO4 battery charging from solar arrays, eliminating central regulator single points of failure.
* **PCB Layout & CSA Reviews:** Laid out multi-layer PCBs in Altium Designer, optimizing thermal dissipation paths and mechanical envelopes for spaceflight. Escorted electrical designs through formal Preliminary (PDR) and Critical Design Reviews (CDR) hosted by panels of engineers from the Canadian Space Agency (CSA) and Nanoracks.
