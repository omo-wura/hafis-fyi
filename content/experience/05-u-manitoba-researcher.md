# Graduate Research Assistant

* **Institution:** University of Manitoba (RIGA Lab)
* **Title:** Graduate Research Assistant / M.Sc. Student
* **Period:** September 2018 - May 2021

## Role Overview
Researched, simulated, and built a grid-connected 400W solar micro-inverter prototype based on a novel single-stage isolated flyback-family topology. Responsible for power stage design, custom magnetics fabrication, real-time control firmware programming, and hardware-in-the-loop validation.

## Core Accomplishments
* **Converter Prototyping & Integrated Magnetics:** Co-invented and prototyped a novel, single-stage galvanically isolated micro-inverter topology with integrated magnetics. Fabricated custom hand-wound integrated magnetics on MnZn ferrite E-core structures, combining transformer and inductor functions to reduce core count.
* **Real-Time Control Loop Coding:** Programmed 100kHz real-time control loops (including PLL grid synchronization and 2p2z average current compensators) in bare-metal C on a TI Delfino F28379D MCU.
* **Loop Stability & Modeling:** Built closed-loop electrical models in PLECS to validate control loop stability. Conducted post-build forensic analysis of layout parasitics and leakage inductance to characterize leakage losses, which guided advanced layout design standards in subsequent commercial roles.
