Hardware Deep Dive 01

PV Optimizer & Rapid Shutdown Safety

In series-loop residential photovoltaic configurations, a single shaded panel dictates the current limit for the entire string. Implementing a PV optimizer allows individual panel MPPT (Maximum Power Point Tracking). However, the critical engineering constraint lies in overvoltage protection and rapid shutdown during grid failures or emergencies.

My recently patented topology (US 12,658,699) outlines an apparatus that utilizes a bypass circuit and advanced DSP control logic. In the event of an anomaly, the optimizer safely clamps the voltage and isolates the panel from the DC bus in milliseconds, satisfying strict NEC rapid shutdown requirements without compromising conversion efficiency during normal operation.

optimizer_isr.c 
// Simplified Interrupt Service Routine for overvoltage protection
void __interrupt pv_voltage_monitoring_isr(void) {
    float v_out = ADC_read(V_OUT_CHANNEL) * SCALING_FACTOR;
    
    if (v_out > OVP_THRESHOLD_VOLTS) {
        // Trigger rapid shutdown sequence
        PWM_disable_switching();
        Relay_engage_bypass();
        
        SystemState.fault_flag = FAULT_OVP_TRIPPED;
        Telemetry_send_emergency_flag();
    } else {
        // Continue MPPT Perturb and Observe
        execute_mppt_algorithm(v_out);
    }
    ADC_clear_interrupt_flag();
}
1

Embedded C control loop typical of TI C2000 microcontrollers. The control loop must execute well within the switching frequency period (e.g., < 10μs for a 100kHz switching stage) to prevent component failure during a sudden open-circuit event on the DC bus.

Hardware Deep Dive 02

The ISOMBI Topology

My master's research culminated in the Isolated Manitoba Inverter (ISOMBI). Traditional grid-connected micro-inverters rely on bulky Low-Frequency Transformers (LFTs) for galvanic isolation. To increase power density, I designed a single-stage topology that integrates magnetic components and utilizes a High-Frequency Transformer (HFT).

$$ P_{loss\_total} = P_{cond} + P_{sw} + P_{core} + P_{copper} $$

By meticulously modeling the switching losses (\(P_{sw}\)) and magnetic core losses (\(P_{core}\)) in PSCAD and MATLAB, the ISOMBI prototype proved capable of injecting power into the grid with near-unity Power Factor while drastically reducing the physical footprint of the inverter module.

2

Single-stage designs mathematically balance the trade-off between the increased complexity of the control scheme against the massive reduction in physical material (copper and iron) required for the system.
